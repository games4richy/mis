Legally Required Business Insurance Policies

Certain insurance policies are mandated by state or federal law, and non-compliance can result in significant penalties, fines, or even business closure .

Required by Law

Workers' Compensation Insurance

Who needs it: Any business with employees—full-time, part-time, or seasonal

Covers: Medical expenses, rehabilitation, and lost wages for employees injured on the job

State variations: Most states require coverage with your first employee, though Texas generally allows opt-out and some states exempt very small employers

Required by Law

Unemployment Insurance

Who needs it: All employers with W-2 employees

Covers: Temporary income for workers who lose jobs through no fault of their own

Funding: Employers pay federal (FUTA) and state (SUTA) payroll taxes

State-Specific

Disability Insurance

Who needs it: Employers in CA, NY, NJ, RI, and HI

Covers: Income replacement for non-work-related illness or injury

Variations: California funded by employees, New York requires employer purchase

Essential Recommended Coverage

Beyond legal requirements, these policies form the foundation of comprehensive business protection against common risks and lawsuits.

Essential

General Liability Insurance

Covers: Customer injuries, property damage, advertising injury lawsuits, and associated legal costs

Who needs it: All businesses with customer interaction—retail, hospitality, service providers

$1M Per Occurrence
$2M Annual Aggregate
Highly Recommended

Business Owner's Policy (BOP)

Covers: Bundles general liability, commercial property, and business interruption insurance

Who needs it: Small to medium businesses, offices, retail stores, restaurants

Benefit: Typically cheaper than buying separate policies and simplifies coverage

Critical

Cyber Liability Insurance

Covers: Data breaches, ransomware, business interruption from digital incidents

Who needs it: Any business handling sensitive data, processing payments, or using cloud platforms

Statistics: 41% of small businesses reported cyberattacks with median cost of $8,300

Key Takeaways

  • Workers' compensation and unemployment insurance are legally required for businesses with employees
  • A Business Owner's Policy (BOP) bundles essential coverage at lower cost than separate policies
  • Cyber liability insurance is no longer optional—41% of small businesses experience attacks
  • Industry-specific risks dictate specialized coverage needs
  • Insurance needs evolve with business growth—annual reviews are essential
  • Working with knowledgeable agents ensures proper coverage for unique business risks

Proper small business insurance isn't just about compliance—it's a strategic investment in your company's resilience and long-term success. By understanding the essential policies and implementing a comprehensive coverage strategy, you protect not just your assets, but your ability to recover and thrive when faced with unexpected challenges.
